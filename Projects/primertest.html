Silicone Primer Adhesion Test (Push & Shear)

We evaluated adhesion strength for silicone‑primed optical assemblies with extended sit times prior to bonding—relevant for larger builds with longer, staged workflows. The goal was to determine whether assemblies remain within acceptable strength margins beyond the typical “prime‑and‑bond within 24 hours” guidance, while identifying environmental factors that most affect performance.

Materials & Variants

  • Optical elements: Multiple lens/glass types (genericized).
  • Housings (“pucks”): Anodized aluminum, chem‑film aluminum, and titanium.
  • Surface preparation: Plasma pen cleaning prior to primer application.
  • Bonding: Silicone primer + RTV adhesive (brand redacted for confidentiality).

Sit‑Time Matrix

Primed components were held for 1, 3, 5, 15, 21, 28, and 45 days prior to bonding to map adhesion vs. sit time. For each interval, we recorded environmental conditions and process timestamps to ensure repeatability.

Test Rig & Procedure

  • Push test on CNC: Programmed a CNC to drive a load pin onto a pressure sensor through a load‑spreading puck to apply force evenly across the glass surface.
  • Shear configuration: Fixtures allowed both normal (push) and tangential (shear) loading modes.
  • Instrumentation: Pressure sensor output logged vs. displacement; pass/fail and ultimate force captured.
  • Process controls: Techs handled surface prep and primer; we recorded temperature, humidity, primer dwell time, bond timing, and cure time for each sample set.

Results

  • Sit time: No significant loss in adhesion strength up to 45 days under controlled humidity for the materials/finishes tested.
  • Material/finish effect: No clear, consistent difference in bond strength across lens types or housing finishes (anodized, chem‑film, titanium) within measurement uncertainty.
  • Dominant factor: Humidity was the primary driver of long‑term primer performance variation.
  • Process reliability: CNC‑driven push testing with a pressure sensor provided repeatable loading and clear failure thresholds for comparisons.

Impact

  • Provided data‑backed justification to continue manufacturing large optical builds with extended primer sit times.
  • Reduced schedule pressure and rework risk by allowing more flexible staging prior to bonding.
  • Standardized prep and logging (plasma clean, primer dwell, environment tracking) improved reproducibility.
