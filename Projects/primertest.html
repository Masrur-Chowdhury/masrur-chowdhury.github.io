Silicone Primer Adhesion Test (Push & Shear)

This project investigated the long-term bond strength of silicone primer–treated surfaces for large optical assemblies, where extended build times can cause primed parts to sit well beyond the manufacturer’s recommended 24-hour limit before adhesive application.

Objective

To determine whether primed surfaces retain sufficient adhesion strength after extended sit times, enabling more flexible manufacturing schedules without sacrificing structural integrity.

Methodology

  • Prepared test samples with primer applied to optical-grade substrates.
  • Allowed samples to sit for various durations: 1, 3, 5, 15, 21, 28, and 45 days before bonding.
  • Performed push and shear tests to measure bond strength at each interval.
  • Controlled and varied humidity levels to assess environmental impact on adhesion.

Results

  • Bond strength remained largely consistent across sit times up to 45 days under controlled humidity.
  • Humidity was identified as the dominant factor in primer performance degradation.
  • Validated safe use of extended sit times for large builds, providing flexibility in manufacturing workflows.

Impact

This testing provided data-backed confidence to proceed with extended primer sit times in certain optical assemblies, reducing schedule pressure and increasing throughput without compromising bond reliability.
